PERFORMANCES

Stories [from], [to], [by] faraway lands Shlok Kaushik

subtitle line 1

byline

2020

An intimate, multisensory performance for 3 audience. A blindfold, headphones and warm hands. Inspired by the mother figures from Kytice by K. J. Erben, we explore the idea of tenderness and care, perception of the known and unknown tickling the mind, and story. imagination etc. fire 300 characters.

+20

820 characters together in 2 paragraphs max. When I first moved to Gurgaon I lived in a building that marked the (then) edge of a rapidly expanding megapolis. Our balcony overlooked a small, rural settlement ‘Ghata’ (meaning loss) with a rocky outcrop of the Aravalli hills in the background. It was not a romantic image; however, there was stillness and withdrawal. My memory of this environment is of heat, dust and manure.

This outdoor vigil, a ritual unfolding over a week, pierced that stillness. The deep hum of chanting, and cymbals beating in hands silhouetted against a lone lightbulb was ever-present. It demanded not being ignored. I painted this scene as a sensory experiment. Premiere:Studio SI, Prague, 2019 Format: One-to-one. Duration: 45 minutes. Language: English or Czech (each audience member can choose) Supported by: SI na Staromáku, Divadlo DISK and Přístav 18600 The performance is part of the NEW NETWORK project in 2020 / New Blood on the Stage 2020
